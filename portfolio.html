// Case Study 01 — Saksham

Taking an HR SaaS from
Zero to First Paying SMEs

Saksham is an HR SaaS built for Indian SMEs — covering payroll automation, attendance & leave management, and employee onboarding. I joined as a founding team member at pre-launch, which meant there was no playbook, no brand, and no pipeline. Everything — GTM strategy, messaging, pricing, and channel mix — had to be built from scratch.

The core challenge was a crowded market where every competitor was selling the same feature list. SME founders weren't looking for software — they were looking for relief from a recurring headache. Our positioning shifted from "HR tool" to "the system that runs your people ops so you don't have to think about it." That framing drove every asset we built.

// Case Study 02 — Tradex

Growing a Prediction Market
Through Community & Creators

Tradex is a prediction market platform targeting sports and fantasy gaming fans in India — a user who already understands the thrill of calling outcomes right, but has never thought of it as a "market." As a founding team member, I owned growth: community building, influencer marketing, referral loop design, and organic/paid campaigns.

The insight that drove everything: fantasy sports fans don't need to be educated on prediction — they already do it. They just needed a platform that felt familiar, competitive, and social. So instead of explaining the concept, we plugged into existing cricket and football fan communities and let the product speak through creators they already trusted.

STEP 01
Community Seeding
Identified and entered 40+ Telegram and Discord groups for IPL, Premier League, and fantasy gaming fans. Seeded Tradex organically through value-first content — match predictions, leaderboards, winner highlights.
STEP 02
Influencer Engine
Built a 3-tier creator programme: nano influencers (campus/local sports creators) for trust, mid-tier sports YouTubers for reach, and Reels creators for virality. Managed briefs, content calendars, and payout structures.
STEP 03
Referral Loop Design
Designed a referral programme tied to match events — users who referred friends before a big game got bonus prediction credits. Loop was timed to natural excitement peaks, driving burst acquisition.
STEP 04
College Campus GTM
Launched a campus ambassador programme at 8+ colleges targeting sports-obsessed students. Ambassadors ran prediction contests within their networks, generating high-LTV users at near-zero CAC.

How I Think

// On Positioning

Most early-stage products fail at positioning because they describe what the product does, not what the user stops suffering through. At Saksham, every SME founder we spoke to wasn't excited about "payroll automation" — they were relieved at the idea of never missing a salary date again. That's the message. Always start with the relief, not the feature.

// On Growth Channels

The best channel is always the one where your user already spends time feeling the problem. At Tradex, that was sports Telegram groups — not ads. At Saksham, it was founder LinkedIn communities. I don't pick channels from a playbook. I find where the frustration lives and show up there with something useful.

// On 0→1 Marketing

At the founding stage, marketing's real job isn't awareness — it's belief building. You're not selling a product yet; you're selling the idea that this team, with this insight, can solve this problem. Every piece of content, every campaign, every community post at Saksham and Tradex was designed to make someone say: "These people get it."
